MathJax Equations

Usage

You can use inline equations with one dollar sign, like this: $ J(x) = \int{L(t, x, \dot{x}) dt} $.
The equation above will render to this: $ J(x) = \int{L(t, x, \dot{x}) dt} $.

You can use equations with auto numbering using two dollar signs. For example, this equation $$ E = m\cdot c^2 \label{eq:mc2}$$ will look like the equation below.
You can refer to it as \ref{eq:mc2} (which will render to this: \ref{eq:mc2}).

$$ E = m\cdot c^2 \label{eq:mc2}$$
